Part 3 – The RAG Identity Update
11 Nov 25 · RAG accuracy and agents

Folding RAG, multi agent ideas, and IBM’s RAG and LangChain work into a clearer “identity” for how I want to use AI in regulated and operational settings.

What is in the toolbox right now
  • VisionAI model trained and deployed to iPhone as an emotion detector
  • Google AI Essentials as a practical safety and productivity foundation
  • Python based training agent that builds AI Weekly Audio Feed briefings
  • Wharton AI for Business Specialization for strategy and leadership framing
  • In progress RAG project focused on accuracy improvement and verification
  • IBM Fundamentals of AI Agents Using RAG and LangChain to deepen the stack
Sharpening the tools
  • Data quality and coverage directly shape RAG answers, no matter how strong the model is
  • Chunking decisions affect clarity, trust, and the ability to verify outputs
  • Evaluation mindset matters, I have to keep checking and adjusting prompts and pipelines
RAG identity and use cases
  • Start with problems where people spend time hunting through long circulars or documents
  • Design systems so every answer points back to the sources in plain language
  • Use a verification layer that can compare and rank candidate contexts before answering
  • Target low monthly costs so the design is realistic for small teams and consulting work

This entry also captures my personal AI principles, such as bias reduction, human in the loop review, and focusing on real ROI instead of shiny use cases.

Part 2 – The Pursuit of Understanding
18 Oct 25 · Making sense of Wharton

Reflecting on the Wharton AI for Business Specialization and how it changed my view of leadership, risk, and real world AI adoption.

Formal training that shaped this phase
  • AI Fundamentals for Non Data Scientists
  • AI Applications in Marketing and Finance
  • AI Applications in People Management
  • AI Strategy and Governance
Key lessons that stuck
  • Even with recent updates, revisiting the content through several models surfaced new angles for today
  • Without a solid grasp of what sits under the hood, it is easy for organizations to stumble on implementation
  • AI work needs strong leadership, change management, and cross functional alignment to avoid multiplying risk
Connecting strategy to hands on projects
  • Mapped course ideas into concrete use cases for marketing, HR, and compliance work
  • Used the readings and frameworks to shape how I define success criteria for my projects
  • Framed AI as one tool inside a broader operating model, not the center of everything

This entry sits between the early VisionAI build and the more technical RAG work, and it explains how formal strategy training influences my design choices.

Part 1 – Strategy to AI/ML and Back Again
30 Sep 25 · First steps into applied ML

The starting point of the journey, where I move from traditional consulting into hands on machine learning and deployment through VisionAI and early agent ideas.

Early toolbox snapshot
  • Trained a supervised VisionAI model for emotion detection and deployed it to iPhone
  • Built a Python based AI training agent to support my learning
  • Started outlining a RAG system concept and a future multi agent research assistant
  • Began the AI for Business coursework with Wharton
What VisionAI taught me
  • Data preparation is hard and has an outsize impact on model accuracy
  • Training the model was easier than getting a smooth, reliable deployment and UI on device
  • Using several models together helps avoid rabbit holes and speeds up problem solving
Looking ahead from this starting point
  • Move from single models to systems that combine retrieval, agents, and evaluation
  • Keep everything grounded in real work problems and measurable value
  • Use this log as a way to show the journey, not just the final demos

This first entry sets the baseline for where I started, so later readers can see how the projects and thinking evolved over time.
